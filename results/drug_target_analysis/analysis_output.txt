================================================================================
INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

The Riker Engine identifies core genes through multi-dataset transcriptomic
convergence (expression-based), not through genetic association or protein
biochemistry. Therefore:

1. EXPECTED HITS: Drug targets whose dysregulation manifests at the
   transcriptomic level (e.g., ABCC8 in T2D, ERBB2/ESR1 in breast cancer,
   JAK2/ITGA4/STAT3 in IBD) should and do appear in core gene lists.

2. EXPECTED MISSES: Targets where the disease mechanism involves:
   - Protein misfolding/aggregation (APP, MAPT in AD)
   - Constitutive expression with gain/loss-of-function mutations (BRCA1/2)
   - Symptomatic treatment of downstream effects (DRD2 in ASD)
   - Cell-type specific expression diluted in bulk tissue (TREM2 in AD)
   These would NOT be expected to appear in expression-based analysis.

3. NOVEL CANDIDATES: Core genes that are NOT current drug targets but show
   strong transcriptomic convergence may represent novel therapeutic
   opportunities worthy of further investigation.

Overall, the Riker Engine recovered 13 of 51 (25.5%) approved drug targets
and 38 of 82 (46.3%) total validated targets across 5 diseases,
demonstrating systematic rather than anecdotal drug target recovery.
